# HR Analytics Dashboard

## Overview
This Power BI dashboard provides a comprehensive view of workforce metrics, attrition patterns, and employee demographics. Built to help HR leaders and business stakeholders make data-driven decisions around retention, hiring, and workforce planning.


## Dashboard Screenshot
![HR Analytics Dashboard](./HR_Analtyics_Final_Dashboard.jpeg)

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Total Employees** | 1,470 |
| **Attrition Count** | 237 |
| **Attrition Rate** | 16% |
| **Active Employees** | 1,233 |
| **Average Age** | 37 years |

## Key Insights

### 1. **Attrition Analysis**
- The company is experiencing a **16% attrition rate** with 237 employees leaving
- **R&D department** shows the highest attrition count, requiring immediate attention for retention strategies
- Sales and HR departments show comparatively lower attrition

### 2. **Workforce Composition**
- **Education Background**: 
  - Majority of employees come from **Life Sciences (42%)** and **Medical (32%)** fields
  - Technical Degree holders represent 10% of the workforce
  - Indicates a STEM-focused organization
  
- **Education Level**:
  - Bachelor's degree holders form the largest segment
  - Strong representation of Master's and Associate degree holders
  - Limited high school and doctoral degree employees

### 3. **Age & Gender Distribution**
- **Age Demographics**: 
  - Largest employee group is in the **35-44 age band** (~450 employees)
  - Second largest group is **25-34 years** (~450 employees)
  - Balanced gender distribution across most age groups
  - Relatively few employees over 55, suggesting potential succession planning needs

### 4. **Compensation Trends**
- **Average Monthly Income by Job Role**:
  - Managers earn the highest average income (~20K)
  - Research Directors and Healthcare Representatives also command premium salaries
  - Sales Representatives and Laboratory Technicians are on the lower end of the pay scale
  - Notable income variation across different job roles

### 5. **Job Satisfaction**
- **Research Scientists** show the highest total satisfaction scores (407 total)
- **Laboratory Technicians** also demonstrate strong satisfaction (369 total)
- **Manufacturing Directors** and **Healthcare Representatives** have moderate satisfaction levels
- Satisfaction ratings distributed across 1-4 scale for each role

### 6. **Department Performance**
- R&D dominates the workforce composition
- All three departments (R&D, Sales, HR) show relatively balanced performance ratings
- Performance appears consistent across departments

## Interactive Features
The dashboard includes interactive filters for:
- **Gender** (Female/Male)
- **Marital Status** (Divorced/Married/Single)

These allow users to slice data and uncover specific insights for different employee segments.

## Business Recommendations

1. **Address R&D Attrition**: With the highest attrition in R&D, conduct exit interviews and implement targeted retention programs
2. **Compensation Review**: Evaluate pay equity across roles, especially for lower-paid positions like Laboratory Technicians
3. **Succession Planning**: With limited employees over 55, develop knowledge transfer and succession plans
4. **Diversity Initiatives**: Ensure balanced representation across education fields and age groups
5. **Retention Focus**: 16% attrition rate suggests need for enhanced employee engagement and career development programs
